    About me

    Hello! My name is Andrew but I mostly go by Co. I am from San Jose, California and am currently a second year student studying my Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering at Santa Clara University.


    Growing up, I always saught interests in creative outlets, project management, and justice-oriented career paths. In high school, I explored creative outlets by picking up photography and digital graphics as a hobby. I created digital flyers for some clubs and was an Outreach Coordinator for a non-profit organization, Interact, where I managed and led community events such as fundraising and volunteering. At that time, I was also interested in the law and how interconnected policies affect societal interactions. With both of my parents being engineers, I naturally geared my future the engineering field as well. Considering my background and interests, I entered Santa Clara University as a Civil Engineering major planning on becoming a Patent Attorney in the future. However, my Silicon Valley roots caught up with me, and after my first Arduino Robot project, I firmly believed the computer science industry was the right path for me.


    Attending LA Hacks 2018 reassured me of this decision. Within 36 hours, my partner and I, who were rudimentary programmers, created a website from scratch that integrated the Google Maps and Twilio APIs. The creativity, teamwork, and problem-solving strategies put into the project kindled my passion for the endless possibilities that the computer science world has to offer. I am planning on creating more side-projects in free time to expand my interests and curiosity in this industry.


    In terms of academics, I am planning on graduating Santa Clara University with a B.S. in Computer Science and Engineering by June 2019, focusing on back-end software development. I am also planning on receiving my M.S. in Software Engineering by June 2020 from Santa Clara University, focusing on Data Analysis and Machine Learning. Currently, in my free time, I am self-learning front-end development, as well as various frameworks for Full-Stack Development.


    I love meeting and working with people, so please feel free to contact me at a17nguyen@scu.edu for more information.
